Agentic AI refers to artificial intelligence systems that can plan, reason, and take actions autonomously to achieve a goal.

Unlike traditional AI models that only respond to prompts, agentic AI systems can:
- Break down complex tasks into smaller steps
- Decide what action to take next
- Use external tools, APIs, or data sources
- Learn from outcomes and adjust their strategy

A common architecture of agentic AI includes:
1. A Large Language Model (LLM) for reasoning and decision-making
2. Memory to store past interactions or knowledge
3. Tools such as search, calculators, databases, or APIs
4. An execution loop that allows the agent to observe, think, and act repeatedly

In real-world applications, agentic AI is used in:
- Autonomous research assistants
- Customer support automation
- Code generation and debugging agents
- Data analysis and reporting workflows
- Personal productivity assistants

Agentic AI systems are especially powerful when combined with Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG).
RAG allows the agent to retrieve relevant information from documents before generating a response.
This improves accuracy, reduces hallucinations, and enables domain-specific intelligence.

LangChain is a popular framework used to build agentic AI systems.
It provides components for:
- Document loading
- Text splitting
- Vector storage
- Tool integration
- Agent orchestration

As agentic AI evolves, it is expected to play a key role in automating complex knowledge work while keeping humans in the loop for oversight and decision-making.
